# AXTI — 2026-04-29 → 2026-05-04 case study

*Written 2026-05-16. Source data: `db/trading.db` (order_decisions, trailing_stop_log,
fundamental_data, signal_log, news_cache, ohlcv_bars, signal_runner_log),
`logs/daily/daily_run_20260501.log` through `20260506.log`, IBKR paper account state
(scripts/open_positions.py).*

## TL;DR

Entered AXTI on 2026-04-29 at $72.96 on a sweep of momentum + analyst-revision +
narrative signals. **Exited 3 trading days later at the bracket take-profit of
$101.19 (38.7%, +$15,470 gross).** The trailing stop never activated — the bracket
TP fired during the first ten minutes of trading on Monday 2026-05-04, well before
the trailing-stop manager's next morning evaluation. Stock subsequently ran to a
$134.00 high on 2026-05-13, so the realised exit captured roughly 46% of the
move-from-entry to peak.

The 2026-05-01 read — **"skillful selection, lucky magnitude"** — survives intact
with one refinement: the *exit mechanism* was the fixed-multiple bracket TP, not
the trailing stop the case study originally hypothesised. That reveals a
structural quirk of the current risk config (3.0× ATR TP vs 2.0× ATR trail
activation) that is worth pinning as a separate enhancement.

## 4. Skill vs luck retrospective

### 4a. Did the trailing stop activate as designed?

**No.** Two things prevented it:

- On Friday 2026-05-01, the close ($88.32) was $3.57 *below* the activation
  level ($91.89). The trailing manager only evaluates once per day in Phase 3.5;
  the *intraday* high of $96.00 — which *did* exceed activation — is irrelevant
  to the manager because it reads daily-bar closes from `ohlcv_bars`.
- On Monday 2026-05-04, the bracket TP at $101.19 fired in the first ~10 minutes
  of trading, before the trailing manager's evaluation at 09:40 ET. By the time
  the manager iterated IBKR positions, AXTI was already flat.

This is a **structural** outcome, not a one-off. With current config
(activation_atr=2.0, trail_atr=2.0, atr_take_profit_multiplier=3.0):

- Activation level = entry + 2.0 × ATR
- TP level = entry + 3.0 × ATR
- Gap between activation and TP = **1.0 × ATR** (~14% on AXTI)

Any move that gaps through both activation and TP between two consecutive
trailing-manager evaluations will trip the TP first. With daily-bar evaluation,
this means **the trailing stop is only reachable on slow moves** — exactly the
moves where you least need it. On a fast move (the kind that produces the
"skill + lucky magnitude" outcome AXTI just delivered), the bracket TP wins.

### 4b. Was the exit price reasonable vs the high-water mark?

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Exit price | $101.19 |
| Post-exit peak (2026-05-13 high) | $134.00 |
| Slippage from peak | **−$32.81 / share = $17,980 left on table** |
| Realised gain captured / peak gain captured | $28.23 / $61.04 = **46.2%** of the peak move |

Post-exit, the stock continued up sharply through 2026-05-13, then pulled back
~16% over 2 days to $112.16 (as of 2026-05-15 close). A purely-hindsight optimal
exit would have been near the $134 peak. A *system-feasible* better exit
would have been a trailing stop converted on 2026-05-01 (had activation been
1.0× ATR instead of 2.0× ATR) — see 4c.

### 4c. Would a tighter / looser stop or activation have changed the outcome?

**Tighter stop / TP**: irrelevant. The position never came close to the original
$54.15 stop. A tighter stop would have created drawdown risk on the entry-day
range ($69.30 low vs $72.96 entry) without changing the realised exit. A
tighter TP (e.g. 2.0× ATR = $92.62) would have exited *earlier* and *lower*,
making the outcome worse.

**Lower activation_atr (e.g. 1.0× ATR)**: would have set activation at $82.05.
2026-05-01 close ($88.32) is comfortably above that. The trailing manager would
have CONVERTED on 2026-05-01, with trail distance 2.0 × ATR = $19.66 from the
new high-water mark. Modelling forward from there (ignoring the fact that ATR
recomputes each day):

- 2026-05-01 close $88.32 → initial trail at $68.66 (below entry — would not
  have stopped out)
- 2026-05-04 high $102.22 → trail ratchets to $82.56
- 2026-05-05 high $110.24 → trail ratchets to $90.58
- ... continues ratcheting through 2026-05-13 high $134.00 → trail at $114.34
- 2026-05-14 low $110.11 → **stops out at ~$114.34**

That is ~$13/share better than the realised exit, *or ~$7,000 additional
profit*, against the cost of widening the activation gate (which would
also engage trailing on weaker moves where the position later reverses to
the stop). The tradeoff is not free; this is **not** a recommendation to
change defaults based on one trade. See "implications" below for the
proposed framing.

**Higher take-profit multiplier**: e.g. atr_take_profit_multiplier=5.0 →
TP at $122.11. Would have captured most of the run but skipped the bracket
exit on 2026-05-04. The system would then have been at the mercy of the
trailing stop (which, with activation at 2.0× ATR = $91.89, *would* have
converted on 2026-05-04 once it next ran — assuming TP didn't fire — and
trailed to a higher exit). This is the more interesting parameter knob.

### 4d. Read shift: skillful selection, lucky magnitude, locked-in exit

The original 2026-05-01 conversation framed this as "skillful selection,
lucky magnitude (+33% in 2 days was timing luck)." The full holding-period
data confirms both halves and adds a third:

- ✅ **Skillful selection** — the universe funnel + ATR sizing put the system
  in a position to capture the move at a sane risk level. The 2.0× ATR stop
  at $54.15 was never threatened.
- ✅ **Lucky magnitude** — +38.7% in 3 days is timing luck. The system's
  thesis was momentum + narrative + sizing, not "this stock will rip 38% by
  Monday."
- 🆕 **Mechanically-locked exit** — the bracket TP fired exactly where it was
  designed to. The system did not "decide" to take profit at $101.19; it
  pre-committed to that price on 2026-04-29. This is **good behaviour** in
  the abstract (removes hindsight bias from exit timing) but it interacts
  with the trailing-stop design (4a) to cap the realised win on fast moves.

Net read: this is **a system success that left money on the table by design**,
not a system success that owes its outcome to luck on the exit side. The
selection skill is real; the exit was rule-driven. If we want a higher
ceiling on outliers like AXTI, the lever is the
TP-vs-activation gap, not "let the model pick exits."

## 5. Implications for the three deferred enhancements

### 5a. Analyst PT revisions — **strengthens the case**

The `fundamental_data` table now contains a clean longitudinal series for AXTI
(table is append-only as of 2026-05-01). Analyst PT trajectory:

| Fetched | Analyst target | Market cap |
|---|---|---|
| 2026-04-30 (entry day rescore) | $67.75 | $5.18B |
| 2026-05-03 | **$87.75** | $6.28B |
| 2026-05-04 → 2026-05-15 | $87.75 (flat through window) | $6.27B → $7.54B |

The Wedbush PT raise ($28 → $80 on Apr 29) was *already* in motion at entry, but
the consensus PT (averaged across covering analysts) didn't reflect it in the
yfinance feed until the 2026-05-03 refresh, where it jumped +30% to $87.75. The
PT then stayed flat through 2026-05-15 even as the stock ran to $134 — so the
"PT revisions lead price" thesis got one cycle of confirmation here and then
saturated. A **PT-delta-as-a-feature** model would have:

- Flagged AXTI on the 2026-04-30 → 2026-05-03 transition as "PT revised +30% in
  one fetch"
- Not flagged anything afterwards (PT flat)
- Would have *added zero* to the entry decision (entry was 2026-04-29; PT
  revision data point landed 2026-05-03, after the move was already locked in)

So this trade is **weak evidence** for the PT-revision factor's predictive
power *at entry* — the entry succeeded *despite* a stale analyst target ($67.75,
below entry price). But it's **strong evidence** for the PT-revision feature as
a **persistence / position-management** signal: a +30% PT bump while a position
is open is exactly the kind of confirmation that should make the system more
willing to *hold* rather than exit early. Today the system exited on a
mechanical TP regardless. A "hold longer when PT is being revised up" rule
would have plausibly produced a larger realised win here.

### 5c. Post-offering survival flag — **strengthens the case**

The 2026-04-24 over-allotment exercise at $64.25 was the key fundamentals
test. The stock entered the AXTI universe on 2026-04-26 (Sunday weekly run)
having already absorbed that dilution event cleanly. Post-entry, $64.25 was
never re-tested — the stock spent the entire holding period and beyond
trading above the offering price. A **"closed above offering price for N
consecutive days post-offering"** flag would have:

- Tagged AXTI as positive on 2026-04-26 (3 days after the offering, all
  closes above $64.25)
- Either bumped its Stage 3 score, or added a feature column to XGBoost

This is the cleanest of the three enhancements to specify: it has an
unambiguous trigger (S-1/424B5 filings + close price relative to offering
price), low data requirement, and an obvious validation set (every secondary
offering in the last 12 months on the universe). AXTI is one positive
example; a few more would justify shipping the feature.

## 6. Additional finding from this case study (not in the original brief)

**The trailing stop is structurally crowded out by the bracket TP on fast moves.**
With current config (activation_atr=2.0, trail_atr=2.0,
atr_take_profit_multiplier=3.0), the trailing manager can only convert if the
underlying moves to the activation level *and stays there long enough for the
next daily evaluation*, which is the same regime where the TP is also nearby.
On AXTI, both happened within a single overnight gap.

Possible interventions, ranked by reversibility:

1. **Lower `trailing_stop_activation_atr` to 1.0** — would have activated the
   trail on 2026-05-01. Cheap config change. Risk: trail activates on weaker
   moves and stops out on normal volatility (the original 2.0× choice was
   deliberately conservative — it sets the initial trailing stop at break-even).
2. **Raise `atr_take_profit_multiplier` to ~5.0** — gives the trailing stop
   room to engage before the TP fires. Larger drawdown risk on positions that
   trigger the trail and then retrace.
3. **Intraday trailing-stop evaluation** — would have caught AXTI's 2026-05-01
   intraday high of $96.00 (above the $91.89 activation). Requires either a
   live data stream during the trading day or a second `signal_runner`
   invocation at, e.g., 15:30 ET. Bigger change.
4. **Replace the fixed TP with a "TP becomes trailing once first-target is hit"
   rule** — i.e. once `current_price >= entry + activation_atr × ATR`, cancel
   the TP and lock in the trail. Conceptually cleanest. Largest code surface.

This finding is worth pinning as a new entry under **Enhancements (open)** in
`CLAUDE.md`. It is not currently captured there. Recommend doing so after one
more outlier trade exits (so the pattern is confirmed across ≥2 cases) rather
than acting on AXTI alone. AXTI is a sample size of one; the conservative move
is to record the observation and revisit after Phase B starts populating
`source='live'` rows in `trade_log` and similar fast-move winners can be
analysed in aggregate.

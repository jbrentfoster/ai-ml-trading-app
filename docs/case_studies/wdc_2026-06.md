## TL;DR

WDC was re-entered 2026-06-12 at $567.36 (70 sh) after its first cycle was circuit-breaker-flattened at a −7.3% loss on the 6/10 mass-flatten, then ran +21.55% in two trading days and exited via its bracket take-profit at $689.62 — a clean, big realised winner (+$8,557.20, +19.8% excess vs SPY). The dominant mechanism is **structural, not skill**: the move from below trail-activation to above the TP happened across two overnight gaps (6/15 +16%, 6/16 +5% at the open), so the once-per-day trailing manager never got a chance to convert the bracket into a trail — the exact AXTI archetype, now confirmed n=2. Two things make this trade a verification event: (1) the exit filled **$16.63 above** the recorded $672.99 TP on the 6/16 gap-up open and was still correctly labeled `exit_reason='tp'` via the directional gap-aware logic — the first **organic** confirmation of the 2026-06-02 MRVL gap-through exit-reason fix; (2) the intraday runner at 15:30 ET on 6/15 *did* observe the activation cross ($646.55 > $646.35) but `intraday_trail_conversion_enabled=False` suppressed the conversion — direct evidence that flipping that flag would have changed this outcome. The single backlog takeaway: WDC strengthens "Trailing stop is structurally crowded out by the bracket TP on fast moves" from n=1 to n=2 **and** supplies the first concrete case where the intraday conversion flag (not just an earlier daily slot) was the deciding lever.

## 4. Skill vs luck retrospective

**1. Did the risk machinery behave as designed?** Yes, on every leg. The bracket was placed GTC with tick-rounded prices; the TP survived the weekend and filled on the gap-up open; the exit-reason waterfall classified the gap-through fill correctly. The trailing manager behaved exactly as configured: the daily 6/15 Phase 3.5 read $630.29 (below activation) from the partial bar, and the intraday 15:30 slot saw $646.55 (above activation) but honored `intraday_trail_conversion_enabled=False` and stayed ratchet-only. Nothing failed. The question is whether the *configuration* was appropriate — see counterfactuals.

**2. Was the exit reasonable vs the post-exit high-water mark?** The TP exit at $689.62 left money on the table in a still-running move: the 6/16 intraday high was $729.92 ($40.30 / 5.8% above the exit) and the close was $706.01 ($16.39 above the exit). So the TP capped the position ~6% below the same-day peak while the stock was still climbing (RSI 75, MACD accelerating). Fraction of the *captured-to-peak* move: ($689.62−$567.36) / ($729.92−$567.36) = 122.26 / 162.56 = **75% of the move to the 6/16 high**. A respectable capture, but the cap is real — this is the structural TP-vs-trail tension, not a clean "optimal exit."

**3. Counterfactuals.**
- **`intraday_trail_conversion_enabled=True` (the deciding lever here).** Unlike AXTI — where no intraday slot ever observed the activation cross — WDC's 6/15 15:30 ET slot *did* log $646.55 > activation $646.35. With the flag on (and the default `intraday_conversion_buffer_atr=0.5` → effective activation $646.35 + 0.5×39.49 ≈ $666.1), the 15:30 print of $646.55 would **still not** have cleared the buffered threshold. So even intraday conversion with the current buffer would NOT have converted on 6/15. Only a *zero-buffer* intraday conversion would have caught it — and that's the riskiest setting (mid-day cancel→place no-stop window). Net: WDC shows the flag is necessary-but-not-sufficient; the buffer would have blocked it anyway. This is a sharper finding than "just turn the flag on."
- **Lower `trailing_stop_activation_atr` (2.0 → 1.0).** Activation would drop to $602.94 (entry + 1×ATR). The 6/15 daily Phase 3.5 read $630.29 — **above** $602.94 — so the daily run would have converted to a trail on 6/15 morning. With a 2×ATR trail distance (~$70), the trail stop would sit ~$560 on 6/15 and ratchet up through the 6/16 gap, leaving the position open to ride past $706 instead of capping at the TP. **This single knob change would have captured the continuing move.** Tradeoff: a 1×ATR activation over-converts on weaker moves that then retrace, surrendering the bracket's fixed TP on names that don't follow through — the AXTI/SNOW docs already flag this.
- **Higher `atr_take_profit_multiplier` (3.0 → 5.0).** TP would move to $743.12, above even the 6/16 high ($729.92) — so the TP would not have fired 6/16, leaving the trail (once activated) to manage the exit. Helps WDC; widens drawdown risk on positions that hit the trail and reverse.

**4. One-sentence read.** **Structural design with a lucky magnitude** — the entry was a skillful, fully-confirmed momentum BUY, but the +21.5% size and the clean TP fill were handed to the system by two overnight gaps that the once-per-day-plus-ratchet-only trail architecture was never positioned to ride; the realised win is real and large, but the system captured the TP, not the trend.

# MRVL — 2026-05-29 entry → 2026-06-02 exit case study

*Written 2026-06-02. Source data: `db/trading.db` (trade_log [**first `source='live'` row in any case study**], fill_log, order_decisions, signal_log, trailing_stop_log, news_cache, fundamental_data, ohlcv_bars, universe_assets), `logs/daily/daily_run_20260529.log` + `daily_run_20260601.log` + `daily_run_20260602.log`, IBKR paper account (DUN329645).*

*This is the first case study built on a **broker-reconciled** trade rather than an inferred exit. Phase B (live-fill reconciliation) shipped 2026-05-29 and this is the **first organic paired round trip** to flow through the daily `reqExecutions` poll end-to-end — the resolution the `docs/reviews/followups.md` 2026-05-29 item was waiting for. The §3 "inferred exit" caveat that every prior study carried is **replaced** here by real `fill_log` / `trade_log` data.*

## TL;DR

The daily runner BUY'd MRVL on 2026-05-29 at $205.09 (192 sh, $40k notional) on the one day in nine that XGBoost flipped positive and let the ensemble clear the gate. **Three trading days later MRVL gapped up +15.5% overnight and the bracket take-profit filled at the open: exit $255.90, +24.77%, +$9,755 realised — the first organic Phase B `source='live'` trade_log row.** The pnl is correct to the penny (canary `pnl + costs_charged == gross` holds: $9,755.23 + $0 = $9,755.23). **But the exit was mislabeled `exit_reason='manual_close'` when it was almost certainly the bracket TP filling on the gap-up open** — the reconciliation's price-match tolerance (~$0.26) cannot span the $11 gap between the recorded TP level ($244.89) and the gap-up fill ($255.90). The single most important finding for the backlog: **`_infer_exit_reason`'s price-match step needs a directional gap-through rule** (a long exit filling at-or-above TP is a `tp`; at-or-below stop is a `stop`), or every gap-through bracket fill — the exact off-session scenario Phase B exists to capture — will be silently misclassified as `manual_close`, polluting exit-reason analytics.

Unlike every prior case study, the exit here is **broker-observed**, not inferred from a position-list diff. `fill_log` records the SELL execution directly. The canary the 2026-05-29 followup specified holds: `pnl ($9,755.23) + costs_charged ($0) == gross ((255.90 − 205.0915) × 192 = $9,755.23)`. ✓

### Why the exit is a TP, not a manual close

The stored `exit_reason='manual_close'` is almost certainly **wrong**. The exit was the bracket take-profit firing on the gap-up open. Converging evidence:

1. **An APPROVED bracket existed** — `order_decisions` shows stop $183.74 + TP $244.89 placed at entry on 5/29.
2. **Consecutive order IDs** — entry BUY is order_id 222, exit SELL is order_id **223**. Bracket children get IDs consecutive with the parent at submission time. A *manual* sell placed on 6/02 (after 4 days and dozens of universe orders) would carry a far higher ID. 223-immediately-after-222 is the signature of a bracket leg submitted on 5/29. (The STP sibling, order_id 224, never filled — OCA-cancelled when the TP filled — so it correctly never appears in `fill_log`.)
3. **Gap-through mechanics** — a sell LMT at $244.89 is marketable against a $253.46 open and fills in the opening auction at the better price; $255.90 is the first executable print. This is textbook "gap-through TP fills at the open."
4. **The price never reached the TP intraday before the gap** — so the TP could not have filled earlier; the only opportunity was the 6/02 open, which is exactly when the SELL executed.

### Why it was misclassified

`execution/reconciliation.py:_infer_exit_reason` runs a 4-step waterfall. For this off-session fill:
1. `order_lookup` — `order_type` was `None` (off-session; the LMT child wasn't in the current session's order book) → skip.
2. `trailing_log` — no CONVERTED trailing row → skip.
3. `order_decisions_price_match` — `tol = max(0.05, 255.90 × 0.001) = $0.256`; `|255.90 − 244.89| = $11.01 ≫ $0.256` → **no match**.
4. `default` — not (MKT + nearby CLOSED_LONG) → returns `("manual_close", "default")`.

Step 3 is the failure. The tolerance is a tight symmetric proportional band that assumes the fill lands *on* the TP level. A gap-through fill lands **above** the TP (for a long) by the size of the gap — here $11 — so the match fails and the trade falls through to `manual_close`. The pnl is unaffected (entry/exit prices are real), but the **exit reason is wrong**, which corrupts every downstream consumer that conditions on `exit_reason` (Page 10 exit-reason donut + per-exit-reason excess table, the `docs/findings/tp_concentration.md` analysis, future realised-Kelly-by-exit-reason).

## 4. Skill vs luck retrospective

### 4a. Did the risk machinery behave as designed?

**Mostly yes, with one classification defect.** The bracket was sized and placed correctly ($40k notional, 2× ATR stop, 3× ATR TP). The TP fired exactly where pre-committed — on the gap-up open it filled at the better price. That is correct, pre-committed behavior (removes hindsight from the exit). The trailing manager behaved correctly too: it never converted because the price was below the $230.28 activation at every evaluation (6/01 high $225.14 < $230.28), and the 6/02 gap happened overnight where no evaluation is possible. The **one defect** is the post-hoc reconciliation's exit-reason label (§3), not the live order logic.

### 4b. Was the exit reasonable vs the high-water mark?

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Exit price | $255.90 |
| Same-day high (6/02) | $277.22 |
| Same-day close (6/02) | $273.00 |
| Slippage from same-day high | −$21.32/sh (~$4,093) |
| Fraction of move-to-same-day-high captured | ($255.90 − $205.09) / ($277.22 − $205.09) = **70.4%** |

The TP exited at the open and MRVL ran another ~7% intraday to $277.22 before closing $273.00. The realised exit captured ~70% of the entry-to-same-day-high move — a far better capture ratio than AXTI's 46% (`docs/case_studies/axti_2026-04.md`), because here the gap-up *was* the move: the TP at $244.89 was already exceeded by the open, so the position couldn't have done better via the TP regardless. (Post-6/02 price action is not yet known; this is a same-day frame only.)

### 4c. Counterfactuals

- **Lower `trailing_stop_activation_atr` (2.0 → 1.0)** → activation $217.69. The 6/01 high $225.14 would have cleared it, so the *daily* trail could have CONVERTED on the 6/01 close ($219.43)… except the daily runner reads daily-bar closes and $219.43 > $217.69, so yes it would convert — trail distance 2× ATR ≈ $25, initial stop ≈ $194. Then 6/02 gaps to $253.46 — the trail ratchets to ≈$228, but **the TP at $244.89 is still live and fills first on the open at $255.90**. Net: **no change** — the TP wins on the overnight gap regardless of activation. This is the AXTI "crowded out" pattern: on an *overnight* gap from below-activation to above-TP, the TP always wins.
- **Raise `atr_take_profit_multiplier` (3.0 → 5.0)** → TP $266.30. On the 6/02 open ($253.46) this TP would **not** fill; the position stays open and rides to the $273.00 close (and the trail, now activated, ratchets up behind it). This is the *only* knob that would have captured more — but it carries the standard cost (positions that hit a higher TP and reverse give back more). MRVL is one data point.
- **Intraday trailing evaluation** → would not help: the move was an overnight gap, invisible to any intraday slot. Only a higher TP captures more of an overnight gap-up.

### 4d. One-sentence read

**Skillful (rare) participation + lucky overnight magnitude + a correctly-firing TP that the reconciler then mislabeled** — the trade is a system success whose only blemish is a post-hoc exit-reason classification bug, not an execution or selection flaw.

### 5a. New finding (primary) — exit-reason waterfall mislabels gap-through bracket fills

The price-match step in `_infer_exit_reason` cannot recognize a TP/stop that fills through a gap. Proposed Outstanding-bugs entry rendered in §5 of the conversation response (fenced block). This is the cleanest actionable finding from the trade.

### 5b. Trailing crowded-out — n=2, overnight-gap subtype

AXTI (2026-05-04) and MRVL (2026-06-02) are now a matched pair: **both** were overnight gaps from below the trail-activation level straight through the bracket TP. The AXTI case study §6 ranked interventions as (1) lower activation_atr, (2) raise TP multiplier, (3) intraday eval, (4) first-target-becomes-trail. MRVL **re-ranks** these for the overnight-gap subtype: lower activation_atr does nothing (TP still wins), intraday eval does nothing (gap is overnight), so only **raise TP multiplier** or **first-target-becomes-trail** can capture more. The CLAUDE.md entry's "do not retune on one trade" gate now has its second fast-move winner — still recommend holding until Phase B accumulates a few more so the TP-multiplier tradeoff can be measured in aggregate, not on two gap-up winners that survivor-bias toward "higher TP would've been better."

### 5c. SELL-bias counter-example

MRVL is a clean counter to the "system is too bearish to ever participate" worry: 8 of 9 sessions were HOLD because XGBoost fought the trend, but the one session it cooperated produced a +24.8% winner. This is consistent with the 2026-05-13 CLAUDE.md note (decide Option B on realised P&L, not BUY:SELL ratio) — and it's a *positive* realised-P&L data point for "leave XGBoost as-is" (the bias is alpha-ish: it gates entries to days the mean-reversion model isn't screaming sell). One data point; not decisive.

### 5d. Minor — live rows written in Phase 1 miss the same-day benchmark backfill

`benchmark_return_pct` is NULL on this row because `run_daily.bat` runs `backfill_benchmark_returns.py` (Step 3b) *before* `signal_runner.py` (Step 4), and the live trade was written in signal_runner Phase 1 reconciliation — after that day's backfill already ran. The row will populate on the next daily run's backfill. This is a one-day lag specific to live rows (WF rows are written by the weekly retrain, before the backfill). Cosmetic; note only.

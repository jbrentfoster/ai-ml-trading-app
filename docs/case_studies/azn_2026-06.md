## TL;DR

AZN was a top-decile momentum pick (Stage-3 score 0.972) bought at $183.37 on 2026-05-07 and stopped out 27 calendar days (~19 trading days) later at $174.70 for a realised **−$1,886.35 (−4.72%)** — and **−7.67% vs SPY** (benchmark +2.95% over the same window). The ATR stop did its job: the loss landed within a hair of the 2×ATR design depth. But the trade is a structural lesson on two fronts. (1) The position **lost its thesis almost immediately** — it rotated OUT of the universe on 2026-05-10, three days after entry — then drifted sideways in a $181–$191 box for three weeks while the model turned **increasingly and correctly bearish** (LSTM saturated to −0.99 by late May), yet the gate never fired a SELL because FinBERT (+0.45) propped the ensemble above the −0.50 SELL threshold. The position had no exit but the ATR stop. (2) On 2026-05-21 the price brushed trail-activation **intraday** ($190.36 print vs $190.08 activation) but the conversion was suppressed because intraday TP→TRAIL conversion is off by default — a breakeven-ish trail there would have exited ~$183 instead of $174.70. The single most important read: **when the model's strongest component (LSTM) is screaming exit on a held long but the gate suppresses the SELL, the ATR stop becomes the only exit — and it is the *worst-priced* one available.**

## 4. Skill vs luck retrospective

**1. Did the risk machinery behave as designed?** Mostly yes. The bracket STP placed at entry − 2.07×ATR fired and capped the loss at −4.72%, almost exactly the 2×ATR design depth (the extra ~$1.30 is open-gap slippage, expected on a stop that triggers at the open). The trailing manager evaluated every single day (33 `trailing_stop_log` rows) and correctly SKIPPED — the daily *close* never reached activation $190.46. **The one design seam:** on 2026-05-21 the 19:30 UTC intraday print was $190.36, above that day's activation $190.08, but `intraday_trail_conversion_enabled=False` (the conservative default) meant the runner logged `intraday: conversions disabled (ratchet-only)` and did nothing. The daily close that day was $189.75 — below activation — so the daily path also correctly skipped. The activation was genuinely brushed intraday and missed by design on both paths.

**2. Was the exit reasonable vs the pre-exit high-water mark?** The post-entry peak was $191.50 (intraday 5/21); the stop exited at $174.70. From peak to exit is −8.8%. But the relevant counterfactual is not the peak — it's *the best system-feasible exit*. AZN closed $185–$189 for the entire back half of May while the model's LSTM was pinned −0.95 to −0.99. **Any** SELL in that window exits ~$185 vs $174.70 — roughly **$2,250 better** than the stop. The stop was the worst-priced exit available for two weeks running.

**3. Counterfactuals (modelled against actual prices):**
- **(a) Honor the model's bearish lean with a SELL.** From 5/26 onward ensemble was −0.33 → −0.42, LSTM −0.87 → −0.99 — but the gate held HOLD because FinBERT (+0.43 to +0.55) dragged the ensemble above the −0.50 SELL threshold. A rule that lets a saturated-LSTM short-circuit the FinBERT prop (e.g. "force SELL when LSTM ≤ −0.95 on a held long regardless of ensemble") would have exited ~5/28 at ~$185 → save ~$2,250. **Tradeoff:** this is the exact inverse of the "Pervasive SELL bias" concern — forcing more SELLs risks whipsawing slow-grinders that recover (GE rode a similar HOLD streak to a +$36 gain). n=1.
- **(b) Enable intraday TP→TRAIL conversion.** Had `intraday_trail_conversion_enabled=True`, the 5/21 19:30 print $190.36 would have converted the bracket to a TRAIL at ~$190.36 − 2×ATR ≈ $183.6 (≈ breakeven). As AZN faded, that trail would have stopped out ~$183–$184 in late May → save ~$1,800 vs $174.70. **Tradeoff:** the cancel-STP→submit-TRAIL sequence opens a sub-second no-stop window mid-session, and the buffer (`intraday_conversion_buffer_atr=0.5`) would actually have *raised* the bar to ~$191.85 that day — so even with the flag on, the 0.5-ATR buffer would have blocked this conversion. AZN argues the buffer is too conservative for low-beta names, but n=1.
- **(c) Breakeven-stop after a near-activation brush.** A "ratchet the stop to breakeven once price comes within 0.2×ATR of activation" rule would have moved the stop to ~$183.4 after 5/21 → exit ~$183 on the fade → save ~$1,850. **Tradeoff:** new mechanism, and it converts shallow pullbacks into exits — would have prematurely killed positions that dip-then-recover.

**4. One-sentence read.** **Structural, with a model-suppression aggravator:** the ATR stop performed exactly as designed and capped a momentum pick that lost its momentum on day 3, but the loss was ~$2,000 worse than necessary because the model's correct bearish conviction (LSTM −0.99) was suppressed by a FinBERT prop and never reached the SELL gate — so a slow, telegraphed decline had no exit but the worst-priced one.

### New finding — model-bearish-but-gate-suppressed longs ride to the ATR stop

```
- **LSTM-saturated-bearish held longs have no exit but the ATR stop when FinBERT props the ensemble above the SELL gate** (`models/signal_gate.py`, `models/ensemble.py`): when a held long's LSTM score saturates strongly negative (≤ −0.95) but a positive FinBERT score keeps the weighted ensemble above the −0.50 SELL threshold, the gate emits HOLD every day and the position has no exit mechanism except the bracket ATR stop — which on a slow telegraphed decline is the worst-priced exit available. Observed 2026-06 on AZN: LSTM pinned −0.95→−0.99 for the final two weeks of the hold while ensemble sat −0.33→−0.42 (FinBERT +0.43→+0.55 dragging it up); the position rode from ~$185 down to a $174.70 stop, ~$2,250 worse than any SELL in that window would have realised. **Proposed change:** evaluate a "conviction override" in `SignalGate.evaluate` — force SELL on a *held* long when LSTM ≤ a configurable floor (e.g. −0.95) regardless of the FinBERT-propped ensemble, OR cap FinBERT's *upward* contribution to the ensemble when it opposes a saturated price-model signal on an open position. **Trigger to revisit:** a 2nd held long stopped out after a ≥5-day LSTM-≤−0.95 + ensemble-HOLD streak (n=2 for the pattern), OR Phase B realised P&L showing the LSTM-saturated-held subset systematically exits worse than a hypothetical SELL-at-saturation. Decide on realised P&L, not the single AZN observation — same discipline as the SELL-bias note (don't tune the gate on one trade).
```

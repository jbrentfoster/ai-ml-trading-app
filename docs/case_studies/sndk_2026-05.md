# SNDK — 2026-05-18 entry → 2026-06-05 exit (trailing-stop case study)

*Written 2026-06-05. Source data: `db/trading.db` (`order_decisions`, `trailing_stop_log`, `signal_log`, `news_cache`, `fundamental_data`, `ohlcv_bars`, `universe_assets`, `fill_log`, `trade_log`), `logs/daily/daily_run_20260518.log` through `daily_run_20260605.log`, `logs/intraday/intraday_run_*.log`. Exit is **Phase-B broker-confirmed** (`trade_log` id=2024, `fill_log` exec `0000dc8f.6aa09ed8.01.01`) — the first trailing-stop case study that does **not** need the inferred-exit caveat.*
*Sister doc to `snow_2026-04.md` (trail-conversion lineage) and `axti_2026-04.md` (the matched "TP crowded out the trail" failure).*

## TL;DR

Bought SanDisk (SNDK) on 2026-05-18 at $1,317.99 (30 sh, $39.5k) near the low of a violent down-bar. The position climbed steadily over the next two weeks; on **2026-05-26 the trailing manager CONVERTED** the bracket into a standalone $211.38 (2×ATR) TRAIL once price cleared activation, **cancelling the original $1,629.94 take-profit**. The trail then ratcheted **30+ times** across daily and intraday slots, riding SNDK to a 2026-06-03 intraday peak of **$1,860.99**, and fired on 2026-06-05 at **$1,648.00** when the price pulled back exactly one trail-width off the peak. Realised **+$9,899.30 / +25.04%** (vs SPY +1.38% over the hold → **+23.66% excess**) — the second-largest single realised win in system history after MRVL.

This is the **cleanest trailing-stop success on record** and the first case where the trail **demonstrably out-earned the bracket TP it replaced** (+$541.80 / +1.37pp over the $1,629.94 TP that would otherwise have capped the trade). The single most important finding for the backlog: the 2×ATR trail width — the parameter the AXTI case tempts us to *tighten* — is exactly what let SNDK survive the 2026-05-27 shakeout (low $1,528.28) to capture the run. A 1×ATR trail would have been whipsawed out on 5/27 at ~+16.5%. **SNDK is direct counter-evidence against lowering `trailing_stop_trail_atr`.**

## 4. Skill vs luck retrospective

**1. Did the risk machinery behave as designed?** Yes — textbook. The trail SKIPPED correctly on 5/19–5/22 (price below the drifting 2×ATR activation), CONVERTED on 5/26 the moment price cleared activation ($1,578.58 ≥ activation), then ratcheted monotonically upward **30+ times** across both the daily 09:35 ET slot and the intraday 12:00/15:30 ET slots. The intraday runner (shipped 2026-05-20) is visibly doing its observability job here — the `RATCHETED` rows at 16:00/19:30 UTC are intraday-slot ratchet detections against live IBKR `trailStopPrice`. The exit fired exactly 2×ATR ($212.99 ≈ $211.38 trail) below the $1,860.99 peak. Every component did what the design specifies.

**2. Exit vs post-entry high-water mark.** Peak intraday $1,860.99 (6/03); entry $1,317.99 → peak gain +$543.00/sh. Captured +$330.01/sh = **60.8% of the peak move**. The 39.2% given back is precisely the 2×ATR trail distance — the structural, by-design cost of a trailing stop. There is no system-feasible exit that captures more *without clairvoyance*: the only bar that printed near the peak was 6/03, and nothing in the data signalled "sell the top" (LSTM stayed +0.98, no SELL gate fired).

**3. Counterfactuals (modelled against actual prices).**
   - **Tighter trail — `trailing_stop_trail_atr` 2.0 → 1.0 ($105.69):** After the 5/26 conversion (peak $1,641.74 → 1×ATR trigger $1,536.05), the **5/27 low of $1,528.28 would have triggered the trail at ~$1,536 = +16.5%** — whipsawed out *before* the entire run. The 2×ATR trigger that day sat at $1,446.62, comfortably below the $1,528.28 low, so the position survived. **The wide trail is the specific reason SNDK captured +25% instead of +16.5%.** This is the cleanest available counter-evidence to the AXTI-motivated temptation to tighten the trail.
   - **Earlier activation — `trailing_stop_activation_atr` 2.0 → 1.0:** would have converted ~5/21 (price $1,542 cleared a 1×ATR activation) instead of 5/26. Net effect on *this* trade is neutral-to-slightly-worse: an earlier conversion with the same 2×ATR trail distance ratchets from a lower peak base and is more exposed to the 5/22 pullback (low $1,473.52). Not obviously better; the cost (more conversions on weaker moves that retrace) is the documented downside.
   - **No conversion (hold the bracket TP):** the original TP $1,629.94 would have filled 5/26 (high $1,641.74, after the 10:07 ET conversion) or 5/27 (high $1,658.77) → **+23.67%**. The trail beat this by **+$541.80 / +1.37pp** and, more importantly, removed the cap that would have ended the trade at $1,629.94 while SNDK still had a run to $1,860.99 ahead of it.

**4. One-sentence read.** **Structural-design success, not luck** — the trailing mechanism converted, ratcheted, and exited exactly as specified, and the 2×ATR width was the decisive parameter that survived the mid-run shakeout to harvest a +25% trail exit that out-earned the bracket TP.

No new Outstanding-bug proposal arises from this trade. The finding is that the **trailing-stop defaults are working** and the AXTI-driven temptation to tighten them is contradicted by SNDK — this should temper, not accelerate, any retune. The existing CLAUDE.md guard ("do not retune defaults until ≥2 more fast-move winners exit and the pattern is confirmed in aggregate") still holds; SNDK is a *grinder* success, not a fast-move winner, so it does not by itself unlock a retune — it argues for *keeping* the current width.

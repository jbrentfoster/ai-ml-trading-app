- **Read: entry was the original sin; exit mechanism was structurally absent.** TMUS is the strongest argument in the dataset for the "HOLD-timeout exit rule" listed in CLAUDE.md's outstanding bugs (under "Correctness — high priority"). A "flatten after N bars without a re-confirming signal" rule would have closed this trade by early May at a smaller loss.

## 4. Cross-case insight: LSTM recency saturation on momentum entries

The three losers all share a feature the AXTI winner did NOT share at entry:
**LSTM was near saturation (≥0.90) at the time of the BUY decision.** AXTI's
entry on 2026-04-29 had no signal_log row preserved (date range gap), but
during the subsequent holding period LSTM ranged from -0.30 to +0.86 — far from
saturated. The losers all entered into LSTM scores near the ceiling.

| Trade | LSTM at entry | XGB at entry | FinBERT at entry | Ensemble at entry | Prior-N-day price action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| UAL | +0.901 | +0.799 | +0.149 | +0.677 | +11.6% in 11 days, +14% in last 4 |
| TMUS | (no signal_log on entry day; +0.994 on 2026-05-07 — 8 days post-entry, by which point price had drifted -1.5%) | varied | varied | (entry was 2026-04-29, signal_log starts 2026-05-07) | +6% gap on 2026-04-28, the day before entry |
| WFC | (no signal_log on entry day; first row 2026-05-07 with LSTM +0.60) | — | — | — | -4.7% pullback from 2026-04-13 peak — entry was buying the decline |

Post-entry, the LSTM behaviour gets really striking. Here are post-entry LSTM
scores as the position bled:

**UAL** (entered 2026-05-08):
- 2026-05-11: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 2.7%)
- 2026-05-12: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.1%)
- 2026-05-13: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.5%)
- 2026-05-14: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 4.1%)
- 2026-05-15: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.4%)

**TMUS** (entered 2026-04-29):
- 2026-05-07 → 2026-05-15: LSTM saturated at **+0.993 every single day** (8 consecutive days) while price fell from $195.82 to $186.83. The score barely changed: +0.99386, +0.99386, +0.99348, +0.99318, +0.99300, +0.99282, +0.99288 — looks like a stuck classifier output, not a model responding to data.

**WFC** had less extreme behavior — LSTM stayed elevated (+0.56 to +0.99) but did show some variation.

### Why this matters

The LSTM target is `sign(5-bar forward return)`. After a sharp rally:
- The 60-bar input window is dominated by recent up-bars
- The model has learned (from the training distribution) "up bars cluster" — momentum is a real edge most of the time
- But the training distribution also contains exhaustion patterns; the model's failure mode is treating *every* extended run as a continuation rather than identifying tops

The near-constant 0.99x score across multiple days **even as price falls** is a
specific failure mode: the input window is essentially the same window
(slides by one bar per day), so a saturated model output stays saturated until
multiple down bars accumulate. By then, the realised loss is locked in.

### What this is *not* enough to act on

This is **three observations** plus AXTI as a counter-example. Don't change
the LSTM. Possible interventions if the pattern holds across more cases:

1. **Add an LSTM-saturation gate.** If LSTM > 0.9 AND the 5-bar return is
   already above the trailing-stop activation level, *require* XGBoost ≥ +0.5
   to confirm (i.e. raise the confirmation bar specifically for momentum-buy
   entries). UAL would have still cleared this (XGB +0.799); TMUS entry-day
   signal isn't preserved so unknowable; WFC would have failed.
2. **Add an "extension" feature to XGBoost.** A `pct_above_20d_high` or
   `pct_above_50d_ma` feature would let XGBoost learn the exhaustion pattern
   that LSTM is missing. UAL entered at 87th percentile of 30-day range; this
   would have been encoded as a feature.
3. **Time-based exit (the HOLD-timeout rule already in CLAUDE.md).** Doesn't
   fix the entry but caps the cost of bad entries. TMUS especially.
4. **A "freshness of LSTM signal" feature.** Penalise BUY signals where LSTM
   has been saturated for ≥3 consecutive bars without price progress — i.e.
   the model is *telling* you it's just outputting noise.

### Trigger to revisit

Wait for either:
- **≥5 total losing trades** where post-entry LSTM stays above 0.9 for ≥3
  consecutive bars while price falls ≥2%, OR
- **Phase B `source='live'` rows accumulate** enough to compute the realised
  hit-rate when LSTM > 0.9 at entry vs LSTM in [0.3, 0.9].

The second is the cleaner statistical test. As of today the answer is "we
don't know, sample size is single digits across both winners and losers."

## Closing

Three different exit mechanisms (stop fired / silent close / still held) across
three different entry archetypes (bought the decline / bought the news / bought
the top). The shared failure mode is at the **entry gate**: all three were
momentum entries where LSTM was near-saturated and the gate didn't have a
"this is extended" feature to push back. The shared fix list is small but
specific (§4): saturation-aware confirmation, extension features, HOLD-timeout,
trailing/TP parameter rebalance.

None of these are AXTI's mirror image — there is no "perfect loser". WFC was
killed by its own stop (working as designed); TMUS escaped without us knowing
quite how; UAL is still alive but pointing the wrong way. Future-me should
add to this file rather than start a new "losers v2" doc when the next
non-winners surface.

#### Skill vs luck retrospective

1. **Risk machinery: worked.** Bracket STP fired cleanly at the right level. No orphan legs, no sign-flips, no NaN-ATR issues. The current Cancel-TP → Cancel-STP → Submit-TRAIL sequence is irrelevant here (trail never activated; activation level was $226.64, never reached).
2. **Exit vs counterfactual:** the *worst point* in the holding period was 2026-05-18 intraday low $197.90 — the stop caught it. There's no "earlier exit" the system could have produced — no SELL signal fired during the holding period (signal_log shows only HOLD and BUY-rejected rows until the 2026-05-18 ensemble flip to -0.453, which was AFTER the stop fired). The stop *was* the earliest possible reasonable exit given the system's signal layer. A HOLD-timeout rule at e.g. 5 bars without re-confirming BUY would have exited 2026-05-14 close $208.59 ≈ -$820 / -2.1% — much smaller loss.
3. **Counterfactual knobs:**
   - **Tighter stop (1.5× ATR instead of 2.0×):** stop at $202.51 instead of $199.01. Would have fired 2026-05-15 (low $200.41 per DB, $200.41 per yf — yf and DB match on 5/15!), realised -$1,960 / -4.9%. Saves ~$650 vs actual outcome at the cost of (a) being more vulnerable to noise stop-outs on other trades (b) UAL would have been stopped 1 day earlier at similar incremental savings.
   - **Saturation gate at LSTM > 0.95 with XGB confirmation ≥ 0.5:** TEL entry LSTM was +0.976, XGB was +0.262. Would NOT have cleared (XGB < 0.5). **This trade specifically would have been blocked.** The trade-off: how many *winning* trades would the same gate also block? Need realised P&L data (Phase B) to evaluate.
   - **"Extension feature" (price vs 20-day high):** TEL entry $212.98 was at the 27th percentile of the trailing 20-day range ($206-$248). Surprisingly **not** an "extension" entry — TEL was the *opposite* of UAL on this axis. An extension-feature gate would have *passed* TEL entry, so this knob is orthogonal to TEL's failure.
4. **One-sentence read: TEL is the loser that broke the "LSTM saturation" framing.** Unlike TMUS/UAL where LSTM stayed pinned at +0.99 throughout the bleed, **TEL's LSTM came down from saturation over 4-5 bars (0.976 → 0.273 → -0.735)** as price actually fell. The model *did* update — but too slowly to generate an exit signal before the stop. This is a different failure mode: not "the model is stuck", but "the model responds but the response is structurally late given a fixed-ATR stop placed 7% away."

### 6. Revised cross-case insight (extends §4)

The four-trade dataset now contains **four distinct entry archetypes**, not three:

| Symbol | Entry archetype | LSTM at entry | Post-entry LSTM behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WFC** | Bought into ongoing pullback (-4.7% from peak) | (no signal_log row preserved) | Elevated +0.56 to +0.99 with some variation |
| **TMUS** | Bought day after +6% news gap | (no signal_log row preserved) | **Saturated at +0.993 for 8 consecutive bars** while price fell -5% |
| **UAL** | Bought *after* a +11.6% rally, on a -2.2% pullback | +0.901 | **Saturated at +0.999/+1.000 for 7 consecutive bars** while price fell -5.4% |
| **TEL** | Bought a 2-bar weak bounce off a -16% multi-week decline | **+0.976** | **De-saturated** over 4 bars (0.976 → 0.273 → -0.735) tracking the actual decline |

**Refinement to the §4 framing**: the failure mode is **not** "LSTM gets stuck near saturation indefinitely." It's narrower than that — LSTM stays saturated as long as **the 60-bar input window remains dominated by the run-up** that preceded entry. Once enough down-bars enter the window, LSTM does come off saturation (TEL is the proof). The lag is the killer: TEL's LSTM took **6 trading days** to move from +0.976 to +0.273, by which point the stop had already fired (or was about to).

Two consequences:
- The "LSTM-saturation gate at entry" intervention (§4 option 1) **is still motivated** — three of four entries had LSTM > 0.9 at entry (only WFC unknown), and TEL's LSTM ≤ 0.95 by day 5 confirms the response IS there if you give it time.
- The "freshness of LSTM signal" intervention (§4 option 4) needs refinement: the failure isn't a stuck classifier output, it's **the input window's slow decay**. A symptom-targeting gate could be "if LSTM was > 0.9 on the BUY bar AND on 4 of the prior 5 bars, require XGB ≥ 0.5" — captures TMUS and UAL, would NOT have blocked AXTI (whose entry-window history isn't preserved but whose post-entry LSTM ranged broadly).

**TEL's failure is NEW** and not covered by any §4 intervention: TEL entered on a *post-decline* technical setup (-16% pullback then 2-day bounce), with LSTM near saturation despite price weakness, RSI at 47 (not overbought), MACD still negative, and **Stage 3 score of only 0.33**. Three of the four signals were "this is a recovery" — the system saw a momentum reversal after a decline. The actual outcome was the decline resuming, not reversing.

A *different* intervention that addresses TEL specifically:
- **Confirmation between LSTM direction and MACD direction.** TEL had LSTM +0.976 (strongly bullish) with MACD -2.76 (negative → bearish). The two disagreed *at entry*. A simple "block BUY when LSTM > 0.7 AND MACD < 0" rule blocks TEL cleanly. AXTI's entry MACD wasn't preserved but the post-entry rally implies MACD was rising. **This is the first specific feature-disagreement gate this dataset suggests.**

### 7. Data integrity finding — stale partial-day bars in `ohlcv_bars`

This is the most actionable finding of the update — independent of the four-trade analysis, this affects every analysis that queries `ohlcv_bars` for "what really happened on day D" for symbols that left the universe.

**Mechanism:**
1. `signal_runner.py` Phase 2 fetches a daily bar mid-day (e.g. 10:50 ET on 2026-05-18).
2. `yfinance.Ticker(sym).history(period=...)` returns whatever the day's *current state* is at fetch time — a *partial* daily bar.
3. `data.fetcher.upsert_bars` writes the partial bar to `ohlcv_bars`.
4. The market closes at 16:00 ET. The bar's *true* O/H/L/C is now known.
5. **Nothing re-fetches the bar.** Next day's `signal_runner` only fetches symbols in (today's universe ∪ today's held-only), incrementally appending the new bar — it does not refresh prior bars.
6. Symbols that drop out of the universe AND aren't held don't get their D-1 bar refreshed at all.

**Observed concrete evidence** (this case study):
| Symbol | Date | DB Low | yf Low | DB Close | yf Close |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| TMUS | 2026-05-15 | 186.77 | **185.10** | 186.83 | 185.22 |
| UAL | 2026-05-18 | 93.79 | **91.36** | 94.34 | 92.55 |
| TEL | 2026-05-18 | 200.80 | **197.90** | 201.56 | 200.51 |

For all three, the DB's "daily low" was *above* the stop level; the *true* daily low was *at or below* the stop. This staleness directly caused the "mechanism unclear" framing in the original §2 TMUS analysis — we couldn't see what actually happened because the DB lied (silently, with no error).

**Downstream impact:**
- Every dashboard that reads `ohlcv_bars` (Page 1, Page 6, Page 10) shows stale bars for symbols outside the active universe.
- Every walk-forward retrain that includes these symbols trains on stale bars (low impact for far-history bars; high impact for the most recent week).
- Every future case study reading from `ohlcv_bars` will be misled the same way unless this is fixed.

**Fix shape** (concrete, see §8 below for the CLAUDE.md proposal):
- Option A — **end-of-day refetch pass**: after market close, refetch all bars for the previous trading day for any symbol with a position OR with a bar dated today. Cheap (~5 sec for 75 symbols). Run from a separate cron at 16:30 ET.
- Option B — **refresh-the-current-day-bar on every fetch**: change `upsert_bars` to overwrite the current day's bar even if it exists. Solves it for symbols that get fetched daily (active universe + held-only) but **doesn't help symbols that drop out of universe + aren't held** — which is exactly the TMUS/UAL/TEL situation here.
- Option C — **detect-and-refetch on read**: cheapest at write time, but pushes the cost to every reader (every UI page, every walk-forward) — bad pattern.
- **Recommended: Option A.**

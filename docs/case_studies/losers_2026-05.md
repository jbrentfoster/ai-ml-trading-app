# Losers review — 2026-04 → 2026-05

*Written 2026-05-16. Sister doc to `axti_2026-04.md` (winner case study).
Three trades chosen by sweeping `order_decisions` for APPROVED BUYs and ranking
by MTM-against-latest-close: WFC (stop fired), TMUS (slow bleed, just closed),
UAL (bought the top — still held). Sources: `db/trading.db` (order_decisions,
trailing_stop_log, signal_log, universe_assets, fundamental_data, news_cache,
ohlcv_bars), IBKR paper account state (`scripts/open_positions.py`,
`scripts/open_orders.py`).*

**Caveat for all three**: realised exit prices are *inferred*, not observed.
Phase B (live IBKR fill reconciliation) is not yet shipped, so `trade_log` has
no `source='live'` rows for any of these. Inferences below rest on
(a) trailing_stop_log gaps, (b) order_decisions `REJECTED_NO_POSITION` after a
SELL signal, (c) absence from current IBKR positions / open orders, and (d)
daily OHLCV bars vs the known stop level.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| TMUS | 2026-04-29 | 195.79 | 186.83 | 185.38 | **Position closed 2026-05-15→16** (mechanism unclear — stop never broken on a regular-hours daily low) | ~-$1,830 / **-4.6%** if exit ≈ last close | ~12 trading days |
| UAL | 2026-05-08 | 100.07 | 94.63 | 91.29 | **Still held**, full bracket alive | -$2,170 / **-5.4%** (unrealised) | 5 trading days so far |

Three different exit mechanisms (or lack thereof), but **one shared pattern at
entry**: every one of these positions cleared the gate while LSTM was extremely
bullish (≥0.90 score, near saturation) on prices that had *just* run up sharply.
The LSTM target is `sign(5-bar forward return)`, and after a sharp rally the
recent 60-bar input window is near a local maximum — the model has effectively
learned "yesterday was an up day, so tomorrow will be too." When the rally
exhausted, LSTM stayed bullish all the way down (UAL post-entry LSTM scores:
0.999, 0.999, 0.999, 0.999, 0.999) while the actual price collapsed. **See
§4 "Cross-case insight: LSTM recency saturation on momentum entries"** below.

### Exit

- **Inferred fill: ~$75.67 on 2026-05-11 open** (gap-down through $75.94 stop). IBKR STP orders become MKT on trigger, so fill is at the next available print after the gap, not at the stop level. May 11 opened at $75.67, dropped to a low of $74.50 before settling at $75.25.
- **Realised loss: (75.67 − 79.93) × 500 = -$2,130 / -5.3%**, or worse with any slippage to the day's $74.50 low.
- Stop did the job it was designed to do — protected the account from the further -3% slide that followed.

### Skill vs luck retrospective

- **Stop placement: appropriate.** Stop at 2.0 × ATR ($79.93 − 2 × ~$2.00 = $75.94) sat below natural support (the 2026-04-15 close of $80.29 was the recent floor, never violated until the May 8 plunge). Tightening the stop would have stopped out on noise in the Apr 27–May 1 window; loosening it would have only deepened the loss when the gap came.
- **Entry timing: poor.** WFC was in a 4-day pullback from $86.64 (2026-04-13) when the system bought on Apr 24 at $79.93 — i.e. the system bought a falling knife. Compare to UAL below, which is the opposite mistake (bought after a sharp rally peaked). WFC is a "bought into the decline thinking it's a dip" case. The 2026-05-08 leg down to $76.76 was the resumption of the dominant trend, not a fluke.
- **News context: mildly negative.** News flow Apr 23–25 had average sentiment around -0.36 to -0.02 (10 articles total) — not catastrophic but tilted negative. FinBERT picked some of this up but the LSTM-XGBoost combination overrode it.
- **Read: clean mechanical loss.** Stop fired, account protected, no system-design issue. The lesson is on entry quality, not exit quality.

## 2. TMUS — the slow bleed (just closed, mechanism unclear)

### Entry

- **2026-04-29 13:52** — BUY APPROVED 204 sh @ $195.79. Stop $185.38, TP $211.40, cost basis $39,941.
- Universe state: Stage 3 score 0.74 (strong), added 2026-04-20 (~9 days before entry), $1.23B avg dollar volume, market cap $209B. **Removed from active universe on 2026-05-03** — held as orphan from that point forward.
- Fundamentals at entry: P/E 20.8, fwd P/E 14.0, profit margin 11.6%, revenue growth 10.6%, analyst PT $263.59 (~+34% upside).
- Entry day context: TMUS gapped up massively on 2026-04-28 (open $185.17, close $185.32 — a +6% move from prior close $174). The entry on 2026-04-29 was *the next day* after that gap, near $195 — i.e. **bought the day after a +6% gap up**.
- News cluster: 53 articles 2026-04-28, 27 articles 2026-04-29 (entry day), average sentiment +0.21 to +0.44 — earnings or major-news driven coverage burst. The system was buying into the heaviest news flow.

### Exit (inferred — mechanism unclear)

- Position is **not** in current IBKR open positions; no open orders remain.
- No SELL CLOSED_LONG decision in `order_decisions` and no `signal_runner_log` row for 2026-05-16 (market closed Saturday).
- The bracket stop was $185.38; the May 15 regular-session low was $186.77 — $1.39 above the stop. So a regular-hours stop fire on May 15 should not have happened.
- **Three plausible mechanisms** (no way to disambiguate without Phase B fills):
    1. **Pre-market or after-hours print on 2026-05-15 dropped below $185.38**, triggering the GTC stop. IBKR brackets default to RTH but `outsideRth` may be set on this account — worth verifying.
    2. **Manual close** by the user via the Page 9 dashboard or `scripts/open_positions.py --close --symbol TMUS` sometime between 2026-05-15 16:36 ET and 2026-05-16 11:34 ET.
    3. **IB Gateway state inconsistency** (Gateway is known to log out overnight per CLAUDE.md). If the position list returned by `reqPositions()` is partial due to a state issue, TMUS could still actually be held and merely invisible to today's query.
- If exit ≈ last known price ($186.83): **realised loss ≈ -$1,830 / -4.6%**. If stop fired pre-market: somewhere between $185.38 and $185.00, **realised loss ≈ -$2,100 / -5.3%**.

### Skill vs luck retrospective

- **Entry timing: even worse than WFC.** Bought the day after a +6% earnings gap, into the heaviest news cluster of the month (80 articles over 2 days). This is *exactly* the kind of "buying excitement at the peak" pattern that ends in a slow bleed when the news catalyst exhausts.
- **No clear exit trigger fired.** The stop wasn't broken in regular hours. The signal log shows post-entry signals oscillating between BUY (which got REJECTED as already holding) and HOLD — never a SELL. The trailing stop was nowhere near activation (would have needed $205.62, never reached — peak holding-period high was 2026-04-30 high $199.64). So the position would have sat indefinitely until either (a) the stop eventually triggered or (b) someone intervened.
- **The "no exit" failure mode is the most expensive one.** In hindsight, an earlier exit at any point May 1 → May 8 would have produced a smaller loss; the system has no mechanism to take such an exit because LSTM stayed pinned at 0.99 throughout (see cross-case insight §4).
- **Read: entry was the original sin; exit mechanism was structurally absent.** TMUS is the strongest argument in the dataset for the "HOLD-timeout exit rule" listed in CLAUDE.md's outstanding bugs (under "Correctness — high priority"). A "flatten after N bars without a re-confirming signal" rule would have closed this trade by early May at a smaller loss.

- **Entry timing: textbook "bought the top tick".** The 4-day +14% rally Apr 30 → May 7 was the dominant feature of the 60-bar LSTM input window; LSTM saturated bullish (0.901), XGBoost crossed from -0.47 to +0.80 in one day (XGB's "RSI now high → mean revert" rule that the SELL-bias diagnosis identified did NOT engage here — UAL's RSI was high but the model voted up; this is itself worth poking at), and FinBERT was neutral. They were REJECTED only because "already holding UAL" — not because the gate said no. If the system had been flat going into those days, it would have *entered then*, in the middle of the decline.
- **Stop and TP are reasonable.** ATR-sized stop at -8.8% gives the trade room without taking ruinous risk. The +13.1% TP is symmetric to the historical 5-bar return distribution. Whether the stop will fire is now a matter of whether the trend continues — May 15 close $94.63 is still $3.34 above stop $91.29.
- **Read: open question.** This trade could still recover. But the *entry* was a model failure: the system saw a clean signal in what was, in hindsight, a chase trade. If it gets stopped out at $91.29, realised loss will be **~$3,500 / -8.8%**.

## 4. Cross-case insight: LSTM recency saturation on momentum entries

The three losers all share a feature the AXTI winner did NOT share at entry:
**LSTM was near saturation (≥0.90) at the time of the BUY decision.** AXTI's
entry on 2026-04-29 had no signal_log row preserved (date range gap), but
during the subsequent holding period LSTM ranged from -0.30 to +0.86 — far from
saturated. The losers all entered into LSTM scores near the ceiling.

| Trade | LSTM at entry | XGB at entry | FinBERT at entry | Ensemble at entry | Prior-N-day price action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| UAL | +0.901 | +0.799 | +0.149 | +0.677 | +11.6% in 11 days, +14% in last 4 |
| TMUS | (no signal_log on entry day; +0.994 on 2026-05-07 — 8 days post-entry, by which point price had drifted -1.5%) | varied | varied | (entry was 2026-04-29, signal_log starts 2026-05-07) | +6% gap on 2026-04-28, the day before entry |
| WFC | (no signal_log on entry day; first row 2026-05-07 with LSTM +0.60) | — | — | — | -4.7% pullback from 2026-04-13 peak — entry was buying the decline |

Post-entry, the LSTM behaviour gets really striking. Here are post-entry LSTM
scores as the position bled:

**UAL** (entered 2026-05-08):
- 2026-05-11: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 2.7%)
- 2026-05-12: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.1%)
- 2026-05-13: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.5%)
- 2026-05-14: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 4.1%)
- 2026-05-15: LSTM **+0.999** (price down 5.4%)

**TMUS** (entered 2026-04-29):
- 2026-05-07 → 2026-05-15: LSTM saturated at **+0.993 every single day** (8 consecutive days) while price fell from $195.82 to $186.83. The score barely changed: +0.99386, +0.99386, +0.99348, +0.99318, +0.99300, +0.99282, +0.99288 — looks like a stuck classifier output, not a model responding to data.

**WFC** had less extreme behavior — LSTM stayed elevated (+0.56 to +0.99) but did show some variation.

### Why this matters

The LSTM target is `sign(5-bar forward return)`. After a sharp rally:
- The 60-bar input window is dominated by recent up-bars
- The model has learned (from the training distribution) "up bars cluster" — momentum is a real edge most of the time
- But the training distribution also contains exhaustion patterns; the model's failure mode is treating *every* extended run as a continuation rather than identifying tops

The near-constant 0.99x score across multiple days **even as price falls** is a
specific failure mode: the input window is essentially the same window
(slides by one bar per day), so a saturated model output stays saturated until
multiple down bars accumulate. By then, the realised loss is locked in.

### What this is *not* enough to act on

This is **three observations** plus AXTI as a counter-example. Don't change
the LSTM. Possible interventions if the pattern holds across more cases:

1. **Add an LSTM-saturation gate.** If LSTM > 0.9 AND the 5-bar return is
   already above the trailing-stop activation level, *require* XGBoost ≥ +0.5
   to confirm (i.e. raise the confirmation bar specifically for momentum-buy
   entries). UAL would have still cleared this (XGB +0.799); TMUS entry-day
   signal isn't preserved so unknowable; WFC would have failed.
2. **Add an "extension" feature to XGBoost.** A `pct_above_20d_high` or
   `pct_above_50d_ma` feature would let XGBoost learn the exhaustion pattern
   that LSTM is missing. UAL entered at 87th percentile of 30-day range; this
   would have been encoded as a feature.
3. **Time-based exit (the HOLD-timeout rule already in CLAUDE.md).** Doesn't
   fix the entry but caps the cost of bad entries. TMUS especially.
4. **A "freshness of LSTM signal" feature.** Penalise BUY signals where LSTM
   has been saturated for ≥3 consecutive bars without price progress — i.e.
   the model is *telling* you it's just outputting noise.

### Trigger to revisit

Wait for either:
- **≥5 total losing trades** where post-entry LSTM stays above 0.9 for ≥3
  consecutive bars while price falls ≥2%, OR
- **Phase B `source='live'` rows accumulate** enough to compute the realised
  hit-rate when LSTM > 0.9 at entry vs LSTM in [0.3, 0.9].

The second is the cleaner statistical test. As of today the answer is "we
don't know, sample size is single digits across both winners and losers."

## Closing

Three different exit mechanisms (stop fired / silent close / still held) across
three different entry archetypes (bought the decline / bought the news / bought
the top). The shared failure mode is at the **entry gate**: all three were
momentum entries where LSTM was near-saturated and the gate didn't have a
"this is extended" feature to push back. The shared fix list is small but
specific (§4): saturation-aware confirmation, extension features, HOLD-timeout,
trailing/TP parameter rebalance.

None of these are AXTI's mirror image — there is no "perfect loser". WFC was
killed by its own stop (working as designed); TMUS escaped without us knowing
quite how; UAL is still alive but pointing the wrong way. Future-me should
add to this file rather than start a new "losers v2" doc when the next
non-winners surface.
